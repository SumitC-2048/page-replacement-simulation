Page Replacement Algorithms

FIFO (First In, First Out)

The FIFO algorithm replaces the oldest page in memory with the newest page requested. It's a simple and easy-to-implement algorithm but may not always provide the best performance, especially in scenarios where older pages are frequently accessed.

LRU (Least Recently Used)

The LRU algorithm replaces the least recently used page in memory with the newest page requested. It's based on the principle that pages that have been accessed recently are more likely to be accessed again in the near future. LRU tends to perform better than FIFO in many scenarios but may require more overhead to implement efficiently.

Optimal Page Replacement

The Optimal page replacement algorithm selects the page that will not be used for the longest period of time in the future. It's a theoretical concept and serves as a benchmark for other algorithms. While Optimal provides the best theoretical performance, it's not practical to implement in real systems due to the need for future knowledge of page accesses.
